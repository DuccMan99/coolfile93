Step 5:=20 Soldering

The first thing you will do is remove the seven screws = holding the=20 circuit board to the case. Place these in a jar or cup so that they do = not get=20 lost. These screws are shorter than the main case screws, so they are = easy to=20 spot the difference, use the same jar if you want then. Also remove the = volume=20 slider and keep it with the screws so that it too does not turn up = missing.

Flop the circuit board over and you are ready to rock. = Note that=20 if you had difficulty getting one or more of the mounting screws on that = this is=20 good time to do that, as you are unfettered with access tot he patch bay = now.=20

Take your 16 20" lengths of wire and strip both ends of = each. On=20 one end you should have a 1/4" or more, fully tinned tip for soldering = to the=20 patch bay and the other should have an 1/8" tip for soldering to the = circuit=20 board. Make you tips nice and solid with solder when tinning, that way = there is=20 minimal heat transfer when soldering to the circuit board.

Work one wire at a time soldering all 16 to the patch = bay end=20 first. Then bundle the wires with zip ties, run the wires under the = circuit=20 board and around the other end. This will keep the wiring from = interfering with=20 the keys or battery compartment, etc.

Replace the circuit board on the case and secure all = seven=20 screws holding it to the case. Find the rear of the right most IC = (furthest away=20 from the patch bay). From bottom left corner of the IC (where the mic = and line=20 inputs are), counting clockwise from the bottom left, place a black dot = adjacent=20 to pins 2, 3, 5, 6, 8, 11, 13, 14, 16, 18, 21,23, 24, 25, 26, and 28. = Since I=20 use color coded wire, I will segregate them into two rainbow looms of 8 = in the=20 same order as the resistor color code: black, brown, red, orange, = yellow, green,=20 blue, purple. That makes all 16 wires for the patch bay, which I wire in = the=20 same order, very easy. Refer to pictures for any questions concerning = this.

Now, solder the 10 ohm resistor across the 1/4" output = from the=20 positive to negative lead. Also solder the white lead from the = ex-speaker to the=20 ground or sleeve of the jack and the one with the red stripe to the = positive or=20 tip lead on the 1/4" jack.

Lastly we will hook up the wiring for the helper = section. Now, you=20 can use different methods in the section from this simple 4 switches all = separate, to array in series, parallel, midi control, trigger control, = etc.=20 Presented here is the simplest setup, it works very well. Basically just = wire as=20 shown, pairs are set up in an under over fashion, just run the wires (8x = about=20 3-4" cut, stripped and tinned) so that the switches close the circuit = between=20 these pairs.

Inspect the case for debris, dirt, solder drops, etc. = Make sure it=20 is all clean, then strip the two leads from the battery compartment if = you=20 disconnected it, re solder to the points they were cut from and close = this=20 sucker up. Put in two screws just to hold it all together, and test it! = Put in 5=20 fresh batteries and hook up headphones to the 1/8" jack or a line out to = the=20 1/4" jack. If the headphones are in they will shut off the 1/4" in the = same way=20 it used to shut off the speaker since you are running off of this. Make = sure all=20 of the normal functions still work and then you can commence placing = patch=20 cables in the patch. Keep in mind that the top 16 sockets are the live = part of=20 the patch connected to the SK-1 circuit board. The bottom 8 are = connected to the=20 switches at the bottom. Now you can use say 1 patch cable to connect = socket 2 to=20 socket 13 or you could use two cables, one to socket 2 and 1/2 of one = helper=20 section and the other cable to socket 13 and the other half of that = helper=20 section. Now the switch connected to that helper can turn the mod = desired on and=20 off. You have 4 pairs of these, so that you can set up and recall = patches.=20 That's about it, from here you have the infinite possibilities of the = tablebeast=20 designed original patch bay SK-1 mod. Pretty sweet. If you have any = questions,=20 please ask them at the FORUM.=
