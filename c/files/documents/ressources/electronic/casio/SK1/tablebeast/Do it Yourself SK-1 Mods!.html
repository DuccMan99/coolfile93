modified Casio SK-1=20 Synthesizer

Step 1: Inspection and Mental=20 Preparation

The first thing you want to do is inspect your Casio = SK-1. Make=20 sure it works before you open it up. Even if it has worked in the past = as far as=20 you know, throw some AA batteries in there and fire it up, check all the = functions and ensure that it is operating before you take it apart. = Nothing=20 would be worse than working on this thing for hours only to put it back = together=20 and have it not work at all. If it works perfectly before you started = the mod=20 and you follow these simple instructions, it will work perfectly when = you are=20 done, its that simple. Oh, yeah feel free to bite it and taste what = quality=20 Japanese workmanship is like. (Click on any pics to enlarge them). Next, = take a=20 deep breath and appreciate how clean the air is. Ventilate wherever you = are=20 working and even turn a fan on low just to circulate the air where you = are.=20 Solder is toxic and can lead to things like eating keyboards.

Step 2: = Assembling the Tools=20 and Parts

Ok, now get all the stuff you will need for the mod = ready. Here is=20 your tool check list.

1. A soldering pencil, = stand for=20 holding it and sponge for cleaning it. 15 to 30 watts is fine, get the = best you=20 can afford if you intend to do this sort of stuff for a while. I have = pictured a=20 super cheap Radio Shack model that has a switch for 15 or 30 watts.

2. Wire cutters / = strippers. Again I=20 have a cheap Rat shack set pictured here. Its all you need. Like any = tools=20 though, you get what you pay for. If you are planning on doing this type = of=20 stuff a lot, get the best you can afford. It will serve you much better = than the=20 cheap stuff down the road.

3. Drill or cutting device. = I use a=20 Dremel with a sheet rock bit on it. Its designed to cut sheet rock and = the like,=20 but it works wonders on plastics (even Aluminum in a pinch). The right = angle=20 adapter makes working on this kid of stuff much easier. I used a cheap = battery=20 powered black and decker (or something) for a little while, but it died = before=20 too long. I bought the Dremel and never looked back. Anyway, anything = that can=20 make holes and cut a fairly straight line will do. I even used a pocket = knife=20 for a while, you can score repeatedly until it cuts through and twist in = a=20 circle for holes, just be careful.

4. A Sharpie. Don't = overlook this. You=20 will need it for marking both on the case and the circuit board. Any = permanent=20 type with a small pointed tip will do.

5. Small phillips = screwdriver. This is=20 vital, you can't even get the SK-1 open without it.

6. Solder, of the rosin = core variety.=20 You can't connect anything without it. I recommend practicing at least a = little=20 if you're a novice. Do this by taking any old piece of non functioning=20 electronics plus some wire leads and practice soldering the leads to = solder=20 joints on it. solder and then once dry give a little tug to the wire, if = it=20 comes off you suck. Try again until you get reasonably good at it.

7. Safety Goggles (not = pictured because=20 I was already wearing them when taking the pics).

8. Additional items not = necessary, but=20 nice to have.. 5/16" nut driver, zip ties, plastic friendly epoxy, = pliers, small=20 chisel, sponge, rosin, headphones, beer.

Here is your parts = checklist

1. 1x Unmodified Casio = SK-1. Do I=20 really need to explain why?

2. 2x 12 point RCA patch = panels. I get=20 these from RP Electronics = in Canada,=20 you can get all the parts and tools you need for this project there as = well,=20 INCLUDING the machine screws.Or you can use any 12 pt. RCA patch panel, = or=20 modify this part of the project for yourself and create whatever kind of = patch=20 bay you want, from banana plugs to a TT patch bay, internal or external, = the sky=20 is the limit.

3. 4x SPST, SPDT, DPDT, = DPDT C-OFF will=20 all work, get what you can as I will show you how to wire up whatever = you=20 choose. The DPDT Center-off variety adds functionality and complexity to = the=20 helper section of the patch panel.

4. 1x 1/4" socket, any kind = will=20 do.

5. About 40 ft. of wire. I = buy new=20 surplus 10' 25 pin D-Sub cables, cut off the ends to use with my = external mod=20 boxes, and then cut 20" lengths of what's left, one for each SK-1.This = wire is=20 far superior for this type of project than just about anything you could = buy,=20 especially when weighed against cost. If you want you can even find and = use just=20 about any computer cable found at the thrift stores, dumpsters, attic, = yard=20 sales, boot sales, etc. and use it. Once you cut the end off, its fresh=20 perfectly preserved wire inside. I used to do this plenty, but buying it = new is=20 actually a lot less trouble when you build as much stuff as I do. The = only real=20 rule is to keep the wire thin. 22 or 24 awg is best, thinner will work = ok if you=20 are careful, but thicker will lead to problems for lack of room for the=20 bundle.

6. 1x 10 ohm resistor. This = is for the=20 1/4" output to replace the load of the speaker, you could even put a = capacitor=20 on there for filtering as well, but I leave that up to you, I like all = the=20 background noise in places, so I just use external filtering.
